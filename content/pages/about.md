heading: About Monarch
intro: >-
  Monarch Events started the way most good things do — at a long table, after
  midnight, with a bottle of something open. Ella and Nina met working the
  line at a pop-up in Bishop Arts in 2023. Within a year they'd cooked a dozen
  dinners out of each other's kitchens, called it a company, and started
  turning people away.

chefs:
  - name: Ella Fullmer
    role: Chef · Savory
    bio_paragraphs:
      - >-
        Ella cooks like someone who grew up eating well and paid attention.
        Raised between Austin and her grandmother's kitchen in Pescadero, she
        trained at Lucia under Chris Hajtmanek and staged at Homewood Kitchen
        in Los Angeles. Her food leans California — vegetable-forward,
        fat-confident, unfussy plating — with whatever is good that week in
        North Texas setting the direction.
      - >-
        She keeps a notebook of every menu she's ever written. She is also
        the one who will remember that your father-in-law doesn't eat
        fennel.
  - name: Nina Ortega
    role: Chef · Pastry & Wine
    bio_paragraphs:
      - >-
        Nina ran the pastry program at Petra & the Beast for two years
        before going freelance. She bakes the way she was taught by her
        mother — by feel — and she couldn't be less precious about it.
        Expect a bay leaf ice cream you'll think about for a week.
      - >-
        She is also Monarch's wine lead. If you'd like pairings, she'll
        build a list from what you already have in the cellar, or source it
        herself through K&L and Domaine Wine Shop.

sourcing_heading: How we source
sourcing:
  - label: Produce
    body: Dallas Farmers Market (Tuesday and Saturday runs), with Eden's Organic Garden for the softer things.
  - label: Meat
    body: Windy Meadows Family Farm (poultry), 44 Farms (beef), and Peeler Farms (pork and lamb).
  - label: Seafood
    body: TJ's Seafood Market for the day's catch, with gulf shrimp and redfish prioritized when available.
  - label: Dairy
    body: Mozzarella Company in Deep Ellum, Veldhuizen for aged cheeses, Lucky Layla for cream and yogurt.
  - label: Bread
    body: Village Baking Co., always — plus whatever Nina has proofed at home that morning.

closing_note: >-
  We don't pretend the supply chain is any of this romantic. But we do know
  the people who grew, raised, and caught most of what ends up on your table,
  and we think you'd probably like them too.
